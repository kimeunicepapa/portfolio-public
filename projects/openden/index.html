Open Den

OVERVIEW

The Open Den community garden website is an information website that promotes the idea of a community garden as a meeting place for sharing, collaborating, and making friends. Also, it’s a great resource for improving food security and healthier diets.

The website promotes local produce, shares recipes on how to use them, and encourages a healthier lifestyle with more time spent outdoors and more vegetable and fruit intake.

YEAR

2022

DURATION

6 weeks

ROLE

TOOLS

PROBLEM

In this era of rapid technological advancement, people spend more time on their devices rather than outdoors. Community gardens could become neglected because people are not even aware they have one nearby. Fresh produce can also go to waste because people don’t know how to use it.

If the website provides information to access a healthier diet and lifestyle by contributing to the community garden, then people can save on food expenses, learn the skill to utilize various ingredients, and make new connections.

USER RESEARCH

Target Audience

My target audience is college students (17-26 yo), from low-income families, who spend most of their time on their phones and computers. They also don’t know how to cook or cook the same things mostly. They can learn how to share, rest their mind from technology overload, work in a team with people from different backgrounds, and grow their own produce.

Calls to Action

The main call to action is the ‘GET INVOLVED’ on the top right corner of the web page. However, there are three ways people can get involved:

  • ‘VISIT’ which provides information about the community garden’s open hours and upcoming events
  • ‘DONATE’ is where people can financially support the garden, donate equipment, or sponsor events
  • ‘SUBMIT RECIPE’ where people can submit their own recipe to share with the community on how to prepare the local produce
